The Seven Mysteries

Medium: Collage


My zine tells the story of a young school girl. The pages follow her journey across school. While it is
somewhat unclear, the story itself follows a rather common Japanese cartoon theme called “The Seven
School Mytseries” This type of theme revolves around seven different paranormal stories that happen
on the school grounds, usually involving haunted areas. It is also common that once someone sees all
seven of these so-called mysteries, an eight secret one appears.

Since it is not clear what these mysteries are in my zine, it is up to the reader to interpret it. The first
page indicates a cheery blossom tree. In these types of stories, it is common to confess behind the
school near a tree. There tends to be a rumor surrounding it, stating that the confession will work if it’s
beside the so-called tree. Page two showcases stairs. While it is hard to visually explain what tends to go
on in this place is that the number of stairs vary each time. The next place is a classroom. Oddly enough,
in the corner of the class there is a floating chair and desk. The fourth page is a collage of the bathroom.
Many different rumors happen surrounding school bathrooms. There can be a stall that is closed, but
mysteriously enough someone is there. Or a few others that are about ghosts or encounters. The next
place our girl visits is a music room. In this room, there is a piano, but no one is playing it. The music
notes indicate that someone is indeed playing it. In page six, someone is waving back to the girl. It
actually is an art statue. Statues are not supposed to move, unless they are alive or haunted. The before
last page is the school roof. The sky looks magical, somewhat unreal. It is filled with clouds of different
colors. We finally fully see the school girl. Her face isn’t there for aesthetical purposes, but it can be up
to interpretation. Is she actually the eight mystery?
