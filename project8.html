How Racism Affects Cathy Park Hong’s Perception of her Identity as an Asian American

Medium: Word


The written piece “How Racism Affects Cathy Park Hong’s Perception of her Identity as an Asian American”
was my final assignment for my last English class in college. It is a text that analyzes Cathy Park Hong’s
essay “Minor Feelings”. The requirements were to make an analysis about any of the essays we read during the
second part of the session. All the texts are non-fiction and are autobiographical. There are topics of bullying,
domestic abuse, racism and more. I believe many of the essays are relatable, and many people would be drawn to the
themes and the techniques many authors use to communicate their stories. However, I chose to write about that essay
because how of it is very personal to me. I relate to Park Hong’s experiences and it made me aware of how there are
actually words to describe certain feelings.

Before writing the text, I decomposed it in a few elements. As the essay covers a lot, I didn’t want my analysis
to be overwhelming and all over the place. I wanted it to be clear and concise. I decided on the key messages I
wanted to convey. The progress of writing the text was very straightforward. It was written on paper in class. I
did not make a rough draft before, but did make a small plan.

Hopefully, my text will be able to shed some light on how Asian-Americans feel. I also desire that readers understand
second-generation Koreans more, and become interested in learning more.
